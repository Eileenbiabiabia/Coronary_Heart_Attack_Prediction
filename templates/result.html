What is BMI and how does it affect me?

Body mass index (BMI) is a person's weight in kilograms divided by the square of their height in meters. BMI is an easy screening method for weight category—underweight, healthy weight, overweight, and obese. BMI does not measure body fat directly but provides a reasonable body fat estimate.

This means eating a wide variety of foods in the right proportions, and consuming the right amount of foodand drink to achieve and maintain a healthy body weight.

What is Cholesterol and how does it affect me?

Cholesterol is a waxy, fat-like substance that your body needs in moderation to be healthy. It's found in all your cells and is made by your liver. Your body uses cholesterol to: Make hormones and vitamin D, Digest fatty foods, and Form protective layers around cell membranes.

Advice for dietary

{% if chol == 1 %}

Eat more fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds. These foods are high in fiber, which can help reduce LDL cholesterol. You can also try:

  • Limiting foods high in saturated fat, like red meat and full-fat dairy products.
  • Avoiding foods with trans fat.
  • Swapping butter for vegetable oil spreads.
  • Switching whole milk to skimmed milk.
  • Eating oily fish, like mackerel and salmon.

What is Blood Pressure and how does it affect me?

Blood pressure is the force exerted by your blood against the walls of your arteries as your heart pumps blood throughout your body; Essentially, it's the pressure of blood flowing through your arteries, and if consistently too high (known as hypertension), it can damage your blood vessels and organs, potentially leading to serious health complications like heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease.

Advice for dietary

{% if bp == 1 %}
  • Low sodium: The primary focus is significantly reducing your daily sodium intake.
  • Rich in potassium: Include foods high in potassium like bananas, potatoes, and leafy greens to counteract sodium effects.
  • Whole grains: Opt for whole grains over refined carbohydrates.
  • Fruits and vegetables: Eat a variety of fresh fruits and vegetables.
  • Lean protein: Choose lean protein sources like fish, poultry, and beans.
